FAQ

Questions we get asked.

Anything not answered here — our team responds within 24 hours.

What incident types can A2A resolve autonomously?+
Defined, repeatable types with known resolution paths — ERP sync failures, API rate limit events, webhook timeouts, record sync delays, data threshold breaches, routine IT ticket types. Complex issues or anything requiring judgment escalates to a human with full context pre-assembled.
How do we define what A2A is authorised to resolve?+
During implementation, you configure the incident types and resolution actions A2A handles autonomously. A2A operates strictly within those parameters — it doesn't improvise or extend its own authorisation.
What's the difference between A2A and RPA?+
RPA automates a predefined sequence. A2A is agent-to-agent communication — one agent detects, communicates context to another, and that agent acts. A2A also operates continuously without human triggers, which RPA cannot do.
Is there always a human in the loop?+
For incidents within A2A's configured scope — no trigger required. For incidents outside scope, or above a severity threshold you define, A2A escalates immediately with full context. You control the boundary.
Can A2A connect to our existing ServiceNow?+
A2A can escalate to ServiceNow via connected integration — opening a ticket with full A2A context pre-populated. A2A handles what it can autonomously and hands off cleanly for what needs human attention.
How does A2A handle recurring incidents?+
The Root Cause Analysis Agent identifies patterns. If the same ERP sync failure happens three times in a week, A2A flags the systemic issue and escalates to IT with full incident history — not just the latest symptom.
How is ZUUZ priced?+
Pricing is structured across three layers — a Platform Foundation fee, Department Module fees (one per team you activate), and Work Credits that only charge when agents complete real tasks. There are no per-seat fees, ever. See current rates, module-by-module costs, and credit pack pricing on our pricing page, or talk to our team — enterprise pricing is always a tailored conversation.
