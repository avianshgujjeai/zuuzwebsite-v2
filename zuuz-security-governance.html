FAQ

Questions we get asked.

Anything not answered here — our team responds within 24 hours.

What does SOC 2 Type I Certified mean, and when is Type II available?+
SOC 2 Type I certification means an independent AICPA-registered auditor has verified that ZUUZ's security controls are suitably designed as of the audit date — covering all five Trust Services Criteria: Security, Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, and Privacy. Type II requires a 6–12 month continuous observation period; we are actively in that period. The full SOC 2 Type I report is available to enterprise customers under NDA. Contact security@zuuz.io.
How does ZUUZ encrypt our data, and what is AWS KMS CMK per tenant?+
Every tenant is assigned a dedicated Customer-Managed Key (CMK) in AWS KMS — no CMK is ever shared across tenants. This means cross-tenant data access is architecturally impossible. Data is encrypted with AES-256-GCM at rest (object store, database, backups) and TLS 1.3 in transit. Each record gets its own Data Encryption Key (DEK), wrapped by your CMK — the plaintext DEK never touches disk. CMKs rotate annually. Enterprise customers can supply their own key material (BYOK). Keys are stored in FIPS 140-2 Level 3 HSMs and never leave AWS KMS.
What authentication methods does ZUUZ support?+
ZUUZ supports SAML 2.0/SSO (Okta, Azure AD, Google Workspace, ADFS), OIDC/OAuth 2.0 with short-lived JWT tokens (1hr TTL), and MFA via TOTP or WebAuthn/FIDO2 hardware keys — required for all user accounts. API integrations use scoped, rotatable keys with expiry enforcement. Service-to-service communication uses mutual TLS (mTLS) with certificate pinning. Sessions expire after 60 minutes and are bound to IP range and user-agent fingerprint for anomaly detection.
What does tamper-evident audit trail mean in practice?+
Every AI action, every agent decision, and every data point at the time of a decision is logged immediately via immutable CloudTrail and application audit logs — they cannot be modified or deleted. Logs are retained for 7 years by default. If a regulatory review requires evidence of what was known at decision time, the trail provides it with timestamp, user context, and full data scope. Customers can stream audit logs in real time to their own SIEM via webhook or S3 export.
Is ZUUZ GDPR and CCPA compliant? What about HIPAA?+
Yes. ZUUZ acts as a Data Processor under GDPR — customers retain Data Controller status. GDPR rights (access, rectification, deletion, portability) are available via self-service portal. Right to erasure is implemented via CMK revocation plus explicit delete API with a 30-day purge. Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) cover international transfers. For CCPA, ZUUZ does not sell or share California residents' personal information; deletion requests are fulfilled within 45 days. A HIPAA Business Associate Agreement (BAA) is available on request. Contact dpo@zuuz.io.
What is the incident response and breach notification SLA?+
ZUUZ commits to notifying affected customers within 72 hours of a confirmed breach per GDPR Article 33. Automated playbooks isolate affected systems within 5 minutes of a confirmed incident. Recovery targets: RTO 4 hours, RPO 1 hour. A detailed incident report is provided within 14 business days. Critical vulnerabilities are remediated within 24 hours; High within 7 days. Annual third-party penetration testing is performed by a qualified security firm.
What customer security controls are available?+
Enterprise customers can: supply their own CMK material (BYOK) for full key custody; restrict platform access to specific IP ranges (IP allowlisting); enforce corporate SSO/SAML for all authentication; stream audit logs in real time to their SIEM; configure custom data retention periods shorter than platform defaults; and designate a security contact for direct breach notifications. The SOC 2 Type I report, penetration test executive summary, and security architecture documentation are all available under NDA — contact security@zuuz.io.
How is RBAC in ZUUZ different from standard RBAC?+
In most tools, RBAC controls which tabs a user can access. In ZUUZ, RBAC changes what the platform looks like entirely — sidebar, modules, agents, KPIs, and visible data all change by role. CEO and Paralegal log into the same platform and have completely different experiences. Roles are tenant-scoped — no permissions cross tenant boundaries. Access reviews are conducted quarterly, and all privilege escalation events are logged and alerted in real time.
What happens to our data if we stop using ZUUZ?+
Your enterprise systems (SAP, Salesforce, etc.) are untouched — ZUUZ connects via API and your data remains in your systems. Workflow audit logs and platform data can be exported before deactivation. You may revoke your CMK at any time, which renders all associated platform data cryptographically inaccessible — a built-in data deletion mechanism. ZUUZ's API connections are then deactivated and your tenant is fully offboarded within the agreed retention period.
Can ZUUZ support our compliance audit process?+
Yes. The one-click Compliance Audit runner surfaces expired contracts, unsigned documents, Legal Hold violations, and access anomalies across all connected systems. The full audit trail is exportable in JSON/CSV format. For formal security reviews, ZUUZ maintains a comprehensive security questionnaire library (CAIQ, VSA, custom) and can provide the SOC 2 Type I report, penetration test executive summary, and full security architecture documentation under NDA. Contact security@zuuz.io to schedule a session with our compliance team.
